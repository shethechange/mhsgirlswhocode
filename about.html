How Milpitas' first all female coding club came to be

‚úèÔ∏èOur Story

Despite the Bay Area being the heart of technology, learning to code still remains a seemingly unreachable privilege to females, particularly low-income students and people of color. To make computer science more accessible, our team founded Milpitas' first all female coding club.

Girls Who Code is a national non-profit organization leading the movement to inspire, educate, and equip girls with the computing skills to pursue 21st century opportunities. Through our Girls Who Code Club, we aim to close the gender gap in technology. Join our weekly club and learn how to build your own website, create a game, or simply write your first line of code.

The first coding club for females (binary and trans-inclusive) at Milpitas High School. Founded in August 2018, the Girls Who Code community provides a safe, supportive environment for young girls to grow both professionally and personally. It is a place for girls to gain leadership positions and develop critical analytical skills while reducing the gender gap in STEM.
